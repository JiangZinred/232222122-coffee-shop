Robusta


Origin and Growth Conditions:
Robusta coffee beans originate from Central and Western Africa and are now also grown in Southeast Asia.
They are more resilient than Arabica and can tolerate a wider range of climates and altitudes, from sea - level up to around 2,000 feet.
They can grow in hotter and more humid conditions and are less demanding in terms of soil quality.
Flavor Profile:
Robusta beans have a stronger, more bitter flavor compared to Arabica.
They have a lower acidity, which gives the coffee a more full - bodied and earthy taste.
The flavor often includes notes of wood, rubber, and a roasted nutty character.
Some people describe the taste as more "robust" and intense.
Caffeine Content:
Robusta beans have a significantly higher caffeine content, usually around 2 - 2.7% caffeine by weight.
This makes the coffee made from Robusta beans a more powerful stimulant.
Uses:
Robusta is often used in instant coffee production due to its high yield and high caffeine content.
It's also used in blends to add body and a strong flavor.
In some espresso blends, a small amount of Robusta can enhance the crema and give the coffee a more intense kick.
